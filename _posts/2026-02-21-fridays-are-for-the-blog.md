I have thought about doing a reflection post on the insane first semester we all had at Brown. That feels like a lot, and I am not sure my thoughts are ordered enough to look back on it yet. Thinking about that post really put me off of writing anything else, so I decided that I should set some intentions here. 

I am a strong believer in the indie web, and little blogs like this are the backbone of what makes it special. In that spirit, I'd like to commit to a post every Friday. The day may change, or like today, Friday may seem a lot like a Saturday. I also aim to have these posts just be a weekly check in, a small public record of my thoughts from week to week. Maybe they will expand beyond that, but starting small fits into the spirit of the small web.

So there is something to post here beyond my own announcement, here are three podcasts I am really enjoying:

1. **If Books Could Kill with Michael Hobbes and Peter Shamshiri** -- This one is really evergreen for me, an instant lesson. Peter and Michael tear apart the latest pop-sci/self-help/dating advice airport book, revealing the misinformation that usually lies beneath them and contributing evidence to their ultimate theory that these are really all just One Book. Two very funny and smart people having a good time while still letting you feel like you are learning something about good research.

2. **Tech Won't Save Us with Paris Marx** -- An interview-a-week podcast looking at the social ramifications of tech. Paris does a great job of bringing together interested in tech, but looking at it ultimately from a sane and human angle. Some topics end up tech adjacent, but I really enjoyed for instance a recent episode on what the Netflix buying Warner Bros. would do to the film industry. A good podcast to cut through the hype. (Also how could you not podcast with a name like Paris Marx??)

3. **Computer Says Maybe with Alix Dunn** -- This is the newest podcast to my subscription list and so I have spent by far the least amount of time with it but love what I have listened to so far. An interview podcast again focused on the public interest in tech. It seems like there is a really great variety of speakers, and I was drawn in due to an episode I listened to with Audrey Tang, Taiwan's Digital Minister. Looking forward to listening to a lot more here!
